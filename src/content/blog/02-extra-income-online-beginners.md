<p>Every week brings a new promise: &quot;make $5,000/month working 2 hours a day&quot;. Every week thousands of people pay to find out the promise was inflated. This guide is the opposite — an honest comparison of the 5 most common online income paths, with a realistic timeline to first dollar and the median income of people who persist past 6 months.</p><h2>How we organized this</h2><p>We rated each path on 4 dimensions:</p><ul><li><strong>Time to first dollar</strong> — how long until you earn the first cent</li><li><strong>Median income for people who persist 6+ months</strong> — not Instagram outliers, the real median</li><li><strong>Upfront investment</strong> — tools, courses, equipment</li><li><strong>Mental effort</strong> — how much daily attention it demands</li></ul><p>[[produto:affiliate-authority-system]]</p><h2>The 5 paths compared</h2><h3>01. Freelance technical skills (design, copywriting, dev)</h3><p><strong>Time to first dollar:</strong> 2-4 weeks</p><p><strong>Median income (6+ months):</strong> $800-2,500/month part-time</p><p><strong>Upfront investment:</strong> low — existing skill + portfolio platform fees</p><p><strong>Best for:</strong> anyone who already has a technical or creative skill from their day job. Lowest barrier to entry, highest income ceiling per hour.</p><h3>02. Affiliate marketing on owned media (blog/YouTube)</h3><p><strong>Time to first dollar:</strong> 3-9 months</p><p><strong>Median income (6+ months):</strong> $200-1,500/month — wildly variable</p><p><strong>Upfront investment:</strong> low ($30-100/month in tools)</p><p><strong>Best for:</strong> people who can write/produce content consistently and tolerate 6+ months without revenue. Best long-term compounding model on this list.</p><h3>03. Print on demand (Etsy, Redbubble, Shopify)</h3><p><strong>Time to first dollar:</strong> 2-8 weeks</p><p><strong>Median income (6+ months):</strong> $100-800/month</p><p><strong>Upfront investment:</strong> very low — design software + platform fees</p><p><strong>Best for:</strong> people with design sense who enjoy iterating on visual concepts. Income ceiling is real (most stores cap below $2k/mo).</p><h3>04. Reselling (eBay, Facebook Marketplace, Vinted)</h3><p><strong>Time to first dollar:</strong> 1-3 weeks</p><p><strong>Median income (6+ months):</strong> $300-1,200/month</p><p><strong>Upfront investment:</strong> medium — inventory capital</p><p><strong>Best for:</strong> people willing to do physical work (sourcing, photography, shipping). Most income-correlated-with-hours model on this list.</p><h3>05. Selling info products on ClickBank</h3><p><strong>Time to first dollar:</strong> 4-12 months</p><p><strong>Median income (6+ months):</strong> $0-300/month — most never reach $100</p><p><strong>Upfront investment:</strong> low to high (depends on traffic strategy)</p><p><strong>Best for:</strong> people who already have an audience or paid traffic skills. Worst path for absolute beginners despite being heavily marketed as such.</p><h2>The pattern across all five</h2><p>The honest truth: every single path on this list takes <strong>at least 60 hours of unpaid work before the first dollar</strong>. Anyone selling you a model that skips this step is selling marketing, not a business.</p><p>The median person who succeeds online has one thing in common — they tolerated several months of input without output. That's the actual barrier. Not skill. Not capital. Not strategy.</p><p>Pick the path that fits your existing skills, accept that the first months will be painful, and check back at month 6 with data instead of feelings.</p>
